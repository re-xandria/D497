About the Island

Discover the charm of Taniti, a hidden gem nestled in the heart of the Pacific. This small tropical island, spanning less than 500 square miles, boasts a diverse landscape that includes pristine sandy and rocky beaches, a peaceful harbor, lush rainforests, and a dramatic mountainous interior home to a small, active volcano. With a vibrant indigenous community of around 20,000 people, Taniti has long been shaped by fishing and agriculture. However, the recent rise in tourism has opened new doors for visitors to experience the island's natural beauty and rich culture. Come explore Taniti, where adventure and tranquility await at every turn!

The power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

Yes, alcohol cannot be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18, although the enforcement of this law is not very strict.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, in rural areas, especially among older residents, very little English is spoken.

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist visitors.

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so visitors should stay cautious.

Yes, Taniti celebrates many national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed on these days. Visitors should plan accordingly.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.
